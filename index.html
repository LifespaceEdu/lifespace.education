We Know What Works in Education

Decades of research validate project-based learning, student agency, social-emotional development, authentic assessment, and relationship-based approaches. Yet most schools struggle to implement them within industrial-era structures, high-stakes accountability systems, and tight budgets.

The question is no longer "what should education look like?" The question is: "What happens when we actually build from best practices as our foundation?"

Our Team

Marcus Sigh

Founder & Educational Designer

Marcus brings 16 years of teaching experience across diverse settings - from elementary classrooms to locked facilities for adjudicated youth with severe mental health needs, including students everyone else had given up on.

Certified in Special Education (B-12) and Elementary Education in Michigan, with provisional Special Education certification in Hawaii (advancing to standard certification 2026-27). His expertise spans Emotional Impairment, Specific Learning Disabilities, and Autism Spectrum.

Marcus has extensive training in anti-bias and decolonial education, bringing pedagogical approaches rooted in educational justice and solidarity with Indigenous sovereignty movements. He developed the Lifespace Education framework during the pandemic, synthesizing decades of research into a coherent, implementable model.
