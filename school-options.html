School Options: Fall 2026 Launch

Lifespace Education is launching research-based schooling for Big Island families in Fall 2026. We're building one of two models depending on seed funding secured by summer 2026.

Join Our Interest List

The Two Pathways

We're committed to launching Fall 2026. The specific model depends on whether we secure seed funding by summer 2026. Either way, you get research-based education implementing the full Lifespace framework.

Option 1: Tuition-Sliding Microschool

(Launching with seed funding)

A demonstration school proving research-based education can operate sustainably at scale.

The Model:

  • 16 students, grades 1–12 (mixed-age learning)
  • 2 lead educators (8:1 student ratio)
  • Dedicated space in Puna, Hawaii
  • Full six-pillar Lifespace framework
  • $0–$12,000 sliding-scale tuition based on household income

What This Funds:

  • Competitive educator compensation (recruiting qualified teachers from public schools)
  • Quality materials, curriculum, and authentic learning experiences
  • Space lease and operational costs
  • Independent outcomes study documenting effectiveness

Why This Matters:

We're not testing whether research-based approaches work—we're proving this model can scale beyond privileged settings. Year 1 establishes proof of concept. By Year 5, enrollment grows to 24 students at sub-public per-pupil costs with full tuition sustainability.

Option 2: Co-op Hub Model

(Launching without full seed funding)

A collaborative homeschool community with pooled expertise and shared resources.

The Model:

  • Homeschooling families working together
  • Shared educational leadership and curriculum design
  • Pooled resources for specialists, materials, and group experiences
  • Lifespace framework guides learning structure
  • Serving grades 1–12 across Big Island

What Families Gain:

  • Educational expertise you shouldn't have to develop alone
  • Collaborative planning and resource sharing
  • Access to specialist instruction (art, science, movement, etc.)
  • Community of families committed to research-based, equity-centered education
  • Structured learning framework without isolation

Cost Structure:

Co-op model runs on shared family contributions for materials, space rental, and specialist fees. Significantly lower cost than private school or individual homeschooling with tutors.

Why This Matters:

Even without full funding, Big Island families deserve access to research-based education. The co-op model removes the isolation and overwhelm of solo homeschooling while maintaining the flexibility and agency families value.

True $0 tuition available for families who need it. Sliding scale calculated using detailed income worksheet—no family turned away for inability to pay.

The Lifespace Framework (Both Models)

Whichever model launches, students develop across six research-validated pillars:

Critical Thinking

Analysis, information literacy, questioning assumptions

Problem Solving

Iterative approaches to novel challenges without predetermined answers

Core Competencies

Efficient, focused instruction in language arts, math, science, social studies (~2 hours daily, then immediate application in projects)

Expression

Clear communication across modalities (written, spoken, visual, built, performed)

Social-Emotional Learning

Self-awareness, resilience, collaboration, ethical decision-making

Project Work

Extended authentic investigations where all pillars integrate

Who This Serves

Lifespace Education is for families seeking:

  • Research-based education that implements what decades of studies already validate
  • Student agency within supportive structure—not complete autonomy, but genuine decision-making
  • Mixed-age learning where younger students learn from older, and older develop leadership
  • Relationship-based culture without rewards, punishments, or coercive classroom management
  • Equity-centered practices that challenge traditional power structures and center diverse perspectives
  • Real-world relevance connecting learning to authentic contexts and community
